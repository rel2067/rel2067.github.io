About MinuteMed.ai

MinuteMed.ai was born in a study room at the University of Miami. Ryan Lee and Trevor Spinelli, two pre-medical undergraduates, kept running into the same frustration: groundbreaking medical research was being published every week, but understanding it required years of specialized training.

Friends and family would ask about studies they'd seen in the news — and even as pre-med students, explaining them clearly was a challenge. MinuteMed.ai exists to bridge that gap. Every article is explained at three levels — plain English, clinical context, and full methodology — so that everyone from a curious patient to a practicing clinician can engage with the same research.

Our commitment: We only publish summaries for articles we can verify. Every article on this site links directly to the original source. We never fabricate author names, journal names, or study findings. If we cannot verify a detail, we do not publish it.

How It Works

1

Source Verification First

We identify articles from top-tier journals — NEJM, JAMA, The Lancet, Nature Medicine, and others — and verify every detail: title, authors, journal, and findings against the primary source before writing a word.

2

Three-Level Summaries

Each article is summarized three ways. Beginner summaries are written for anyone. Intermediate summaries assume clinical familiarity. Pro summaries provide methodology, endpoints, and statistical detail for researchers.

3

Direct Source Links

Every article links to its PubMed or journal page so you can read the primary source yourself. We are a starting point, not a replacement for the original research.
