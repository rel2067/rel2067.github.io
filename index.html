About MinuteMed.ai

Making medical research accessible to everyone — from curious patients to practicing physicians.

Medical research shouldn't be locked behind jargon. Whether you're a curious patient, a pre-med student, or a seasoned specialist — you deserve to understand what science is discovering about health, right now.

— Ryan Lee & Trevor Spinelli, Co-Founders, University of Miami

Our Story

MinuteMed.ai was born in a study room at the University of Miami. Ryan Lee and Trevor Spinelli, two pre-medical undergraduates, kept running into the same frustration: groundbreaking medical research was being published every week, but understanding it required years of specialized training. Friends and family would ask about studies they'd seen in the news — and even as pre-med students, explaining them clearly was a challenge.

So they built something about it. MinuteMed.ai uses artificial intelligence to generate three distinct summaries of every published paper — one for the general public, one for students and clinicians, and one for specialists who want full methodological detail. The goal isn't to replace medical education; it's to give every person a meaningful way in.

How It Works

1

Literature Monitoring

Our system continuously monitors top-tier journals — NEJM, JAMA, The Lancet, Nature Medicine, Science, and hundreds more — flagging newly published studies as they appear.

2

AI Summarization

Each paper is processed by a large language model that generates three summaries: Beginner (accessible to anyone), Intermediate (for students and clinicians), and Pro (full methodological depth for specialists).

3

Quality Review

Summaries are reviewed for accuracy, with attention to not overstating findings, preserving key nuances, and flagging study limitations.

4

You Choose Your Level

Every reader picks the depth that fits their background and can switch levels instantly on any article. A parent, a nursing student, and a cardiologist can all read the same paper in their own language.
