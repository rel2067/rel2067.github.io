About MinuteMed.ai

Making medical research accessible to everyone — from curious patients to practicing physicians.

Medical research shouldn't be locked behind jargon. We believe that whether you're a curious patient, a nursing student, or a seasoned specialist, you deserve to understand what science is discovering about health — right now.

— Ryan Lee & Trevor Spinelli, Founders

Our Story

MinuteMed.ai was born in a study room at the University of Miami. Ryan Lee and Trevor Spinelli, two pre-medical undergraduates, kept running into the same frustration: groundbreaking medical research was being published every week, but understanding it required years of training. Friends and family would ask about studies they'd seen in the news — and even as pre-med students, Ryan and Trevor struggled to explain them clearly.

So they built something about it. MinuteMed.ai uses artificial intelligence to automatically generate three distinct summaries of every published paper — one for the general public, one for students and clinicians, and one for specialists who want the full methodological detail. The goal isn't to replace medical education; it's to give every person a way in.

Both Ryan and Trevor are currently pursuing their undergraduate degrees at the University of Miami with aspirations of attending medical school. MinuteMed.ai is their attempt to democratize medical knowledge before they even have M.D.s after their names.

How It Works

1

Literature Monitoring

Our system continuously monitors top-tier journals including NEJM, JAMA, The Lancet, Nature Medicine, and hundreds more, flagging newly published studies daily.

2

AI Summarization

Each paper is processed by a large language model trained to produce three distinct summaries: Beginner (accessible to anyone), Intermediate (for students and clinicians), and Pro (full methodological detail for specialists).

3

Quality Review

Summaries are reviewed for accuracy, with particular attention to not overstating findings, preserving statistical nuance, and flagging limitations researchers identified.

4

You Choose Your Level

Every reader picks the depth that matches their background — and can switch instantly. A parent, a student, and a cardiologist can all read the same paper at their own level.
